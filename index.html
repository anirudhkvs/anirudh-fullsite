About Me

I hold a PhD in Theoretical Particle Physics from Northwestern University and an MBA from NYU Stern, and have worked as a management consultant at McKinsey & Company.

My focus is on blending physics-based reasoning, business clarity, and interpretability—using math and code as tools to advance conceptual thinking, not as ends in themselves.

Across my work in quantitative research and AI/ML systems, I aim to uncover hidden structure in financial and signal data while making that structure understandable, actionable, and relevant to real-world decision-making.

Upcoming Research Projects

Tone-Deafness Detection in LLMs

Developing archetype-based evaluation for LLM outputs by modeling signature behaviors—such as synonym richness or quantitative tone—and building mechanisms for uncertainty resolution when user intent is ambiguous.

Option Greeks as Market Beliefs

Reinterpreting Delta and Rho as expressions of market beliefs rather than mechanical sensitivities—comparing classical models like Black-Scholes and Heston with data-enriched approaches to map belief gaps across scenarios such as rate hikes or earnings shocks.

Multi-Spin Structure Modeling Using Potts Models

Prototyping higher-spin Potts models to capture richer agent behaviors and regime shifts in financial markets—moving beyond binary-state dynamics to reflect real-world market complexity.

Relativistic Latent Geometry in Energy-Based Models

Prototyping Energy-Based Models with non-Euclidean latent manifolds inspired by general relativity—modeling shocks and structural changes as curvature distortions in learned metric spaces to improve robustness, alignment, and interpretability in complex data settings.

Multi-Source News Coverage as Market Signal

Analyzing news coverage density and article length as proxies for market visibility and pricing dynamics—modeling price responses conditioned on how many distinct sources report on an event, the depth of coverage, and sentiment strength to capture information diffusion and delayed impact opportunities.
